MARKET MAKING AND VALUATION

Market makers expect price retracement in order to make money on open “Short Sale” positions (positions that are to be covered within minutes or hours) these positions account on average for approximately 40% - 60% of market volume. Sample stats on Short Sale Volume for AAPL as reported to FINRA:

WHY OBSERVE MARKET MAKERS?

Market makers don't have a multi day outlook and they have a breaking point.

LIT MARKET vs DARK POOLS

Market makers can post their desired orders on Level II screens (the “lit” market) showing size and direction or transact in Dark Pools (“Off-Exchange”) where they typically hide trade size and direction. They usually trade on every venue available to hide intention. Level I is the best bid / best offer or the top line of Level II screens.

Mechanics: ALL transactions that are completed “Off-Exchange” have to get reported within 10 seconds to the Trade Reporting Facility (“TRF”) of the NYSE or NASDAQ during market hours.

MARKET STRUCTURE

  • Regulation National Market System (“NMS”) 2005 obliterated old exchanges
  • Equities can trade “on” any exchange or “off” exchange with an omnipresent price
  • Order Protection Rule - intermarket price -created the National Best Bid & Offer ("NBBO")
MARKET STRUCTURE - DISAGGREGATION

OFF EXCHANGE - ATS ( DARK POOLS )

Alternative Trading Systems (aka “dark pools”) are venues that offer institutions the ability to move large blocks of stock with minimal price impact and at lower transaction costs than exchanges. Dark pools operate as crossing-mechanisms. General characteristics of a dark pool:

  • Trades specify direction and quantity(not visible to participants) but not price
  • Buyers and sellers are matched in order of arrival
  • Lack of pre-trade transparency, all orders are hidden until after execution [Resting or Immediate/Cancel (“IOC”) orders]
  • When buy/sell orders are matched, the execution price is typically inside the National Best Bid and Offer and reported with a delay
  • Dark pool orders are less likely to be filled faster than exchange “lit” orders
  • Dark pools do not contribute to the NBBO as their trades are reported after execution
OFF EXCHANGE - OTC

  • Internalizers (Retail / Wholesale) : Trades by retail investors via discount platforms or wealth managers (“wholesaling”), often executed internally or with other internalizers.
  • Central Risk Books : Banks and market makers have internal books to execute trades that off-set or unwind market making risk and optimize trading across desks.
  • Block Trading : Traditional high-touch service matching large buyers and sellers
